How to Read Your Battery Label

LiPo batteries use a compact label format that tells you everything you need to know. A typical label looks like this:

4S 2200mAh 35C

MarkingMeaningExample
4S4 cells wired in Series — multiplies voltage4 × 3.7 V = 14.8 V nominal
2200mAhCapacity in milliampere-hoursCan deliver 2.2 A for 1 hour (theoretically)
35CDischarge rating — safe continuous current multiplier2.2 Ah × 35 C = 77 A max continuous

Key Terms

TermDefinition
S (Series count)Number of cells connected in series. Each cell adds its nominal voltage. A 3S LiPo = 3 × 3.7 V = 11.1 V.
P (Parallel count)Number of cells connected in parallel — increases capacity (mAh) but not voltage. This calculator assumes 1P; multiply capacity for parallel packs.
mAh (Capacity)Milliampere-hours. How much energy the pack stores. 1000 mAh = 1 Ah.
C-rating (Discharge)The maximum safe continuous current, expressed as a multiple of capacity. Exceeding it causes overheating, puffing, and fire risk.
C-rate (Charge)The safe charging current, expressed as a multiple of capacity. 1C is the standard safe maximum for LiPo. Charging at 2C or above requires manufacturer confirmation.
DoD (Depth of Discharge)Usable capacity percentage before you must stop. Discharging beyond 80% DoD repeatedly shortens pack life significantly.
Nominal voltageThe “nameplate” voltage under normal load — the number printed on the pack.
Full charge voltageThe voltage at 100% state-of-charge. Your charger’s CV phase targets this.
Storage voltageIdeal voltage for long-term storage (~50% charge). Storing at full charge degrades the cells.
Cutoff voltageAbsolute minimum per cell. Dropping below this causes irreversible damage and is a fire hazard when recharging.

Safe LiPo Charging — Electrical Engineer’s Checklist

Lithium polymer batteries store enormous energy in a small package. When treated correctly they are safe and reliable. The rules below are non-negotiable. This calculator exists to help you stay within them.

1
Charge at 1C or below unless the manufacturer explicitly states otherwise. For a 2200 mAh pack, that means 2.2 A max. Higher rates generate heat, accelerate aging, and can cause thermal runaway.
2
Always use a balance charger. Balance charging monitors each cell individually and keeps them within ±0.01 V of each other. An unbalanced pack is a ticking bomb.
3
Never leave charging batteries unattended. Most LiPo fires start during charging. Stay in the room. Keep a smoke detector nearby.
4
Charge in a fire-safe container. Use a LiPo safety bag, an ammo can (with the seal removed), or a ceramic/stone surface. Never charge on carpet, wood, or near flammables.
5
Set the correct cell count and chemistry on your charger. Charging a 3S pack on a 4S setting will overcharge every cell to 5.6 V — catastrophic failure is guaranteed. Double-check before you plug in.
6
Never discharge below the cutoff voltage. For LiPo: 3.0 V/cell under load, 3.2 V/cell resting. Over-discharged cells develop internal copper dendrites that cause internal shorts when recharged.
7
Store at storage voltage (3.8 V/cell for LiPo). Full charge storage degrades the electrolyte. Storing fully discharged is equally destructive. Aim for 3.8 V/cell (~50 %).
8
Inspect packs before every use. Puffed, dented, or punctured cells must be discharged and disposed of. Never charge a physically damaged LiPo.
Warning signs of trouble: excessive heat during charge, hissing sound, swelling/puffing, or voltage that won’t rise. If you observe any of these, disconnect immediately and move the battery to a safe area.
